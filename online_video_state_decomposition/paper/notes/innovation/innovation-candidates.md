# Innovation Candidates

| ID | Candidate | Gap versus prior work | Evidence path | Main risk | Status |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| I1 | Replace visual/video attention with BCCB plus low-rank and sparse terms | Would extend Circulant Attention with residual paths | Attention fit and zero-shot replacement | Current Qwen/ViT evidence is negative and oracle routes require dense attention | rejected |
| I2 | Use structured plus low-rank attention as the primary online-video-generation accelerator | Could combine periodic geometry with semantic residuals | Wan denoising loss, quality, NFE, cache, precision, and kernel benchmarks | Few-step causal generation, VMonarch, MonarchRT, RoPeSLR, Forcing-KV, and quantized full-system stacks already occupy the dominant space | deferred to component-level |
| I3 | Test a two-timescale fixed-capacity state with exact recent evidence and unsupervised low-dimensional long-term memory | Probe-first mechanism attribution and exact-recent versus consolidated-memory budget allocation are not established by the closest memory methods | Cross-encoder state probes, matched-byte online tasks, causal memory ablations, and measured tail latency | Reconstruction objectives may not preserve query-relevant or rare evidence | rejected in Oja form |
| I4 | Apply DRE-BCM to compress the router, memory projections, and kernel generator after the state model is validated | Connects parameter-space compression to a validated state-space system | Matched accuracy, bits, and measured kernel latency | Adds confounding before the state hypothesis is established | deferred extension |
| I5 | Exact recent evidence plus query-conditioned learned semantic and episodic memory under a fixed byte budget | Retains the robust recent-cache result while targeting the task-transfer failure of query-free Oja compression | Native video-VLM training or adapter tuning, delayed-query and rare-event slices, matched-byte baselines, and measured read/write cost | May collapse to CausalMem, FOLIO, SelectStream, or semantic retrieval unless the mechanism and systems accounting are sharper | preservation passed; router open |

## Selection Notes

- Chosen next candidate: `I5`, mapped to claims `C1-C3`.
- Reason: it preserves the exact-recent evidence that survives task testing and directly targets the failed query-free consolidation mechanism.
- 2026-07 frontier correction: FOLIO, SelectStream, SAVEMem, MuKV, Tensor Memory, STC, and V-Rex narrow the novelty to mechanism attribution, matched budgets, and complete system accounting.
- Rejected framing: `I1` confuses attention fitting with online state dynamics.
- Deferred framing: `I2` is more crowded and should be revisited only as a component inside a few-step causal, cache-aware, low-precision generation stack.
- Deferred extension: `I4` must not be used as evidence for temporal low rank.
- 2026-07-17 task update: `I3` fails its promotion gate. Across 200 MVBench CLIP examples, `instant_oja` never improves exact recent by the required 0.03 and is 0.04 worse at capacity 16.
- 2026-07-17 LLaVA update: a fixed history-plus-recent frame policy also fails at both 128 and 512 visual tokens. Spatial fidelity has a larger positive point effect than the tested temporal allocation.
- Immediate target: train a query-conditioned bounded memory/readout on top of exact recent evidence. BCCB is a negative control, BTTB is optional, and sparse event routing remains excluded until semantic supervision exists.
- 2026-07-17 execution update: the first gate uses a query-free bounded writer plus question-conditioned top-k/MMR readout. It is calibrated on 100 disjoint examples and evaluated on 200 different examples after excluding the previous formal subset.
- 2026-07-17 formal update: the preregistered diverse-writer plus MMR selector fails, 0.470 versus 0.475 exact recent. Full-history MMR reaches only 0.480, so retaining the complete CLIP history does not solve the task.
- The calibrated option-aware selector chooses zero option weight. This proxy therefore provides no evidence that answer-option contrast should be part of the method.
- A simpler recent-pool top-k selector reaches 0.495, but this was identified after evaluation and reverses its weak calibration ranking. It is being tested once on 200 untouched reserve examples with all choices frozen.
- 2026-07-18 confirmation update: frozen recent-pool top-k remains positive on 200 untouched reserve examples, 0.515 versus 0.500 exact recent, but its +0.015 paired gain has interval [-0.015, 0.045] and is concentrated in scene transition.
- A 48-byte four-feature ridge readout trained only on the original calibration split transfers more consistently: +0.025 on formal evaluation and +0.030 on untouched reserve. Its 400-example descriptive pooled gain is +0.0275 with 13 better and 2 worse decisions, but gains remain limited to action sequence and scene transition.
- `I5` remains selected only for a genuinely learned or native VLM/KV memory if the reserve confirmation supports query-conditioned selection. Handcrafted CLIP retrieval is not itself the proposed contribution.
- 2026-07-18 LLaVA transfer update: the frozen learned readout reaches 0.510 versus 0.470 exact recent on the same 200 reserve examples, a +0.040 paired gain with interval [0.010, 0.075]. It improves three tasks and does not reduce the other two.
- The current ridge readout is not yet superior to strong query-only selectors: +0.010 versus recent-pool top-k and +0.005 versus recent-pool MMR, with both intervals crossing zero. The supported mechanism is query-conditioned access to a larger bounded history, not the optimality of this specific four-feature ranker.
- `I5` now advances to a native learned-memory experiment with matched persistent bytes. The raw-frame anchor uses 24.14 KiB selection state for query policies versus 12.05 KiB for exact recent, plus an unbounded source-video replay path, so it does not yet satisfy complete-state accounting.
- Claim boundary: replaying selected raw frames into LLaVA validates evidence selection only. It is not a bounded deployment result until the visual-evidence cache or re-encoding path is included in state and latency accounting.
- 2026-07-18 native update: every policy receives the same 8,413,328-byte state, including a 16-frame projected feature cache, and query-time source-video replay is eliminated. The learned readout reaches 0.510 versus 0.475 exact recent, a +0.035 point gain with interval [0.000, 0.070] and p=0.0923.
- The native result supports continuing I5 but weakens any claim about the specific learned ranker: learned versus top-k remains +0.010 and learned versus MMR +0.005, with both intervals crossing zero.
- The next falsifiable mechanism is a calibration-only low-rank native feature codec plus sparse high-residual token events. It is a compression probe, not a novelty claim for low-rank plus sparse decomposition.
- The 8 MiB cache and 8.68-second mean write show that bounded state alone is insufficient for deployment. Compression ratio and write latency must improve while preserving the matched-state LLaVA gains.
- 2026-07-18 compression update: rank-256 plus four residual tokens reduces steady-state memory to 1.024 MiB and preserves 99% of learned-policy predictions, but its one-sided 95% loss-event bound is 2.35% and therefore misses the 2% non-inferiority gate.
- The query-conditioned effect remains positive at matched compressed state: learned 0.505 versus exact recent 0.465, +0.040 with interval [+0.010, +0.070] and McNemar p=0.0215.
- Fixed sparse residuals help selectively rather than uniformly. They recover the latent-only action-sequence loss and part of the scene-transition loss, while one learned state-change error remains. Adaptive event allocation is now the selected next mechanism within `I5`.
- `I4` remains deferred. The compression confirmation supports low-rank state coding as an engineering component but provides no evidence that BCCB should replace attention or that structured-plus-low-rank decomposition is a new general principle.
- The parallel hidden-state codec probe independently rejects Fourier as a preferred temporal predictor and rejects its hardened logic controller. Selected residual-PQ points remain useful representation baselines, but the combined controller does not reach the `<0.30` encoder-required-rate gate.
- 2026-07-22 independent update: the frozen routed rank-256 state matches full LLaVA accuracy at 0.450 over 300 untouched examples and passes the 2% preservation gate with a 1.571% upper loss bound. This promotes representation preservation, not the error-oracle router.
- Query-conditioned access remains promising but open: learned routed memory gains +0.020 over exact recent with eight better and two worse samples, but the interval touches zero and McNemar `p=0.1094`.
- The route is spatial-first (75.7% grid frames) with task-dependent sparse fallback. The next falsifiable I5 step is a cheap causal router trained on calibration only and replicated on a second model or benchmark with rare-event and SLO-aware measurements.
- BCCB remains excluded from the promoted claim. A local BTTB/BCCB writer path may be tested after the causal router, but the independent codec result does not establish structured spatial transport or universal attention replacement.
